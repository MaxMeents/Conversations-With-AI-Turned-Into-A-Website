The Strategy: "The Station Method"
To succeed, bring everything to you. Do not stand at the counter.

Clear a space at a kitchen table or put a tray on your lap.
Bring all ingredients, a bowl, and utensils to your seat in one trip (or ask someone to bring them).
Do all assembly while sitting.
The only "standing" time is walking to the fridge or putting a plate in the microwave.
1. The "Dump" Turmeric Chicken (Slow Cooker)
Time on feet: 30-45 seconds (to put pot in base and turn on)

This relies on pre-cut frozen vegetables and boneless meat so there is zero chopping. Turmeric is a powerful anti-inflammatory.

Ingredients:
Chicken breast or thighs (boneless).
1 Bag of frozen mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn) or frozen cauliflower florets.
1 Can of coconut milk (full fat or light).
1 tsp Turmeric powder.
Salt and pepper.
Method:
(Seated) Open the cans and bags. Put the chicken, frozen veggies, coconut milk, and spices into the removable crockpot insert. Stir it once.
(Standing) Walk the insert to the heating base. Turn it on Low for 6-8 hours or High for 4 hours.
Eat it directly from a bowl; no sides needed.
2. Omega-3 Salmon & Avocado Plate (No Cook)
Time on feet: 30 seconds (retrieving items from fridge)

Fatty fish and avocados are staples of the anti-inflammatory diet. Canned wild-caught salmon is precooked and requires zero effort.

Ingredients:
1 Can or pouch of wild-caught salmon (boneless/skinless is easiest).
1 Ripe avocado.
Lemon juice (bottled is fine).
Whole grain crackers or cucumber slices.
Method:
(Seated) Open the salmon pouch.
Cut the avocado in half and remove the pit.
Squeeze lemon over the salmon.
Eat the salmon with a fork and scoop the avocado right out of the skin with a spoon. Use crackers/cucumbers for crunch.
3. The "Grocery Store" Charcuterie Board
Time on feet: 45 seconds (gathering items)

This is about assembling ready-to-eat items that fight inflammation (nuts, berries, healthy fats).

Ingredients:
Handful of Walnuts (high in Omega-3s).
Handful of Blueberries or Strawberries (high antioxidants).
Pre-cooked hard-boiled eggs (buy them pre-peeled in a bag at the store).
Hummus.
Baby carrots (pre-washed/cut).
Method:
(Seated) Put a handful of each on a plate. That’s it. It is a balanced meal with protein, healthy fats, and fiber with zero cooking.
4. Overnight Oats (Berry & Chia)
Time on feet: 30 seconds (putting jar in fridge)

Dairy can be inflammatory for some; this uses almond or oat milk. Oats reduce C-reactive protein (a marker of inflammation).

Ingredients:
1/2 cup Rolled Oats (quick oats get too mushy).
1 tbsp Chia seeds (crucial for Omega-3s).
1 cup Almond milk or Oat milk.
Frozen berries (blueberries/raspberries).
Dash of cinnamon.
Method:
(Seated) Combine all ingredients in a jar or bowl. Stir.
(Standing) Put it in the fridge.
In the morning, just grab it and eat cold. No heating required.
5. Microwave Sweet Potato "Boat"
Time on feet: 60 seconds (2 trips to microwave)

Sweet potatoes are rich in Vitamin A and lower inflammation better than white potatoes.

Ingredients:
1 Sweet Potato (scrubbed).
Canned Black Beans (rinsed).
Salsa (check label for no added sugar).
Optional: Pre-made Guacamole cup.
Method:
(Standing) Poke holes in potato with a fork. Microwave for 5–7 minutes until soft. Sit down while it cooks.
(Seated) Slice the potato open.
Pour cold black beans and salsa right into the hot potato (the heat of the potato warms the beans). Top with guacamole.
6. Rotisserie Chicken Salad
Time on feet: 0 seconds (if you have the chicken at the table)

Store-bought rotisserie chickens are the ultimate energy saver.

Ingredients:
Pre-cooked Rotisserie Chicken.
Bag of pre-washed spinach or mixed greens.
Olive oil and Apple Cider Vinegar.
Pumpkin seeds (Pepitas).
Method:
(Seated) Pull meat off the chicken with a fork.
Toss it into a bowl with the greens.
Drizzle generously with olive oil (very anti-inflammatory) and a splash of vinegar.
Sprinkle seeds on top for crunch.
Shopping List Tips for Low Energy / Low Inflammation
To make this work, your grocery list must focus on convenience items that don't contain inflammatory additives (like high fructose corn syrup or soybean oil).

Frozen is your friend: Buy frozen berries, frozen mixed veggies, and frozen chopped onions. They are flash-frozen at peak nutrition and require no washing or chopping.
Pre-cooked Proteins: Canned salmon/tuna, rotisserie chicken, pre-boiled eggs, and canned beans (BPA-free cans if possible).
Healthy Fats: Avocados, olive oil, nuts (walnuts/almonds), and seeds (chia/flax/pumpkin).
Spices: Turmeric, ginger, garlic powder, and cinnamon are the "big four" for fighting inflammation. Buy them in jars so you don't have to chop fresh roots.
